Step-by-Step Operations

1

Order Entry (Registry)

Log the customer's PO number, reference, and requested items. This initiates the digital footprint of the order.

2

Technical Study (Engineering)

Define the Bill of Materials (BoM). System reserves current stock automatically while flagging missing parts for Procurement.

3

Financial Gating (Finance)

Automated margin checks. Low-margin orders are blocked until Management provides a manual release with justifications.

4

Component Sourcing (Procurement)

Request RFPs from vetted suppliers. Award tenders and issue Supplier POs based on cost and lead-time analysis.

5

Material Intake (Reception)

Suppliers deliver parts. Reception module receives items into Inventory, instantly reserving them for the assigned Factory job.

6

Production (Factory)

Production starts floor release. As units are finished, they are recorded, and inventory is consumed in real-time.

The Road to Fulfillment

1. Finished Goods: Items move from the factory floor to the Product Hub, triggering readiness for billing.

2. Invoicing & Logistics: Finance generates the Tax Invoice and requests the official Gov E-Invoice. Simultaneously, Shipment dispatches goods via POD verification.

Fulfillment Trigger: (All Items Delivered) + (E-Invoice Uploaded) = Order Archived
